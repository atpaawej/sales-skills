# Email & DM — First Touch Patterns

Loaded by `/outreach` when the founder needs first-touch messages.

---

## Channel decision

Ask the founder: "Email or LinkedIn DM?"

- **Email**: Best for cold outreach to professionals, higher message capacity
- **LinkedIn DM**: Best for warmer connection, personal brand building, gate-question technique

---

## Email: 4-Sentence Formula

Every email follows this exact structure:

| # | Sentence | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Who you are | Plain English intro |
| 2 | Why them | Specific to their situation — reference something real |
| 3 | What you offer | Value, not features |
| 4 | The ask | Lowest possible commitment |

### Three variations by psychological hook

**Option A: Curiosity**
- Subject: "Quick question about [their specific situation]"
- Sentence 2 creates a gap they want to close
- Sentence 4 asks a low-friction question

**Option B: Pain-first**
- Subject: "Still dealing with [specific pain]?"
- Sentence 2 names the pain in their language (from your research)
- Sentence 4 offers a way out

**Option C: Social proof**
- Subject: "[Similar company/reference]"
- Sentence 2 references a similar situation or company
- Sentence 4 implies "others like you found this useful"

### Quality check

| Check | Option A | Option B | Option C |
|---|---|---|---|
| **P**ersonalized — references something specific? | | | |
| **A**udience-aware — sounds like you understand their world? | | | |
| **I**mmediate — scannable in 5 seconds? | | | |
| **N**ext step — clear what to do? | | | |

## LinkedIn DM: Gate-Question Technique

Based on Alex Berman's method. 40%+ reply rates.

**Message 1: Connection Request (300 chars max)**

```
Hi [Name], [specific compliment about their work]. Would love to connect.
```

Rules:
- Reference something specific — a post, comment, article, or mutual connection
- Zero self-promotion
- 300 characters max

**Message 2: First DM (gate question)**

Wait 24-48 hours after they accept. Then:

```
Thanks for connecting, [Name]. I really liked your take on [specific insight].

Quick question — are you still dealing with [specific pain] at [company]?
```

The gate question must be:
- Easy to answer (yes/no or quick clarification)
- Relevant to their world (not your product)
- Low-friction: no pitch, no call request

**Message 3: If they engage**

Only introduce your product after they've replied:

```
Since you mentioned [pain], this might be relevant — I built [product] to [specific outcome]. No pressure, but if you're curious: [link or 5-min call ask].
```

---

## Completion criterion

Messages are written, personalized, and validated by the founder. The founder knows exactly what to send and when.
