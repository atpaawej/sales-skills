# Follow-Up Sequence Patterns

Loaded by `/outreach` when the founder needs a follow-up sequence.

---

## Sequence structure

5 touches across channels. 80% of sales require 5+ touches; 44% of salespeople quit after 1.

**Touch 1 (Day 0): Initial message**
Already written — via cold email or LinkedIn DM.

**Touch 2 (Day 3): New angle / value add**

```
Subject: Re: [original subject]

Hi [Name],

Following up — I know you're busy.

I wanted to share [specific resource] that might be useful regardless of fit.

[1-2 sentences on why it's relevant to their situation]

No need to reply if this isn't the right time.

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Touch 3 (Day 7): Social proof**

```
Subject: Re: [original subject]

Hi [Name],

Thought this might interest you — [similar company/person] was dealing with [same problem] and [specific result] after [action].

[1-2 sentence story linking their situation to the outcome]

Worth a quick chat to see if you'd see similar results?

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Touch 4 (Day 14): Break-up**

```
Subject: Re: [original subject]

Hi [Name],

I've sent a few emails and haven't heard back — completely understand.

I'll stop following up for now. But if [specific problem] ever becomes a priority, the door is open. No catch.

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Touch 5 (Day 30-45): Re-engagement**
Trigger-based — reference something new.

```
Subject: [trigger event]

Hi [Name],

Saw that [trigger — funding, launch, post, role change]. Made me think of our earlier conversation — might be worth revisiting.

Let me know.

Best,
[Your name]
```

---

## Cross-channel strategy

| Touch | Channel | Content |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Email | Initial message |
| 2 | Email | Resource share |
| 3 | LinkedIn | Like/comment on their post, then DM |
| 4 | Email | Break-up |
| 5 | Email or LinkedIn | Re-engagement |

---

## Completion criterion

5 complete touches are written (not placeholders). Founder knows when to send each one and on which channel.
